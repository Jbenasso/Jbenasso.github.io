Sea Otters

Sea otters are fascinating marine mammals known for their playful behavior and remarkable intelligence. They are often seen floating on their backs, using rocks as tools to crack open shellfish. These creatures play a vital role in maintaining the balance of kelp forest ecosystems, making them a keystone species. Their presence helps keep sea urchin populations under control, allowing kelp forests to thrive. They're also absolutely adorable!

Beyond their ecological importance, sea otters are also admired for their unique behaviors and adorable appearance. They have the densest fur of any animal, which protects them from cold ocean waters. Sea otters are highly social, often seen resting in groups called rafts. Fun fact: they hold hands with their partner when they sleep so they don't drift away while resting. Despite facing threats from pollution and hunting in the past, conservation efforts have helped many sea otter populations recover.

Fun Facts About Sea Otters

  • They can hold their breath for up to 5 minutes while diving.
  • Sea otters use tools like rocks to open shellfish.
  • A group of otters resting together is called a "raft."
  • Otters choose a favorite "rock" that they keep in a pouch under their arms, which they use as a tool. Otters exchange these rocks with their life partners as a sign of partnership.
